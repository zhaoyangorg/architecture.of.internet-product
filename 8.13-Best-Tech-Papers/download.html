    Hints for Computer System Design

    • Butler Lampson

    9th ACM Symposium on Operating Systems Principles | October 1983

    Published by ACM | Organized by ACM

    ACM Operating Systems Rev. 15, 5 (Oct. 1983). A slightly revised version was reprinted in IEEE Software 1, 1 (Jan. 1984), pp 11-28

    View Publication | View Publication | View Publication

    ACM SigOps Hall of Fame award 2005. A classic study of experience building large systems, distilled into a cookbook of wisdom for the operating systems researcher. As time has passed, the value of these hints has only grown and the range of systems to which they apply enlarged.
    Download BibTex

    Studying the design and implementation of a number of computer has led to some general hints for system design. They are described here and illustrated by many examples, ranging from hardware such as the Alto and the Dorado to application programs such as Bravo and Star.
